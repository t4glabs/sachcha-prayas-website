Empowering Lives, Transforming Communities!

The story of our organisation, Sachcha Prayas Sanstha is one that is rooted in the wide-spread displacement caused by the construction of the Bargi dam in Jabalpur, Maharashtra. People from over 184 villages were uprooted and displaced, causing a loss of homes and livelihood. Our founder, Parvez Khanâ€™s family is one such family that were displaced and put through the turmoil of rebuilding their lives in the name of development. Drawing inspiration from the thousands of affected families, including his own, Parvez committed his life to empower marginalised communities. This paired with his years of journalistic pursuits in the area, highlighting the struggles of the rural people, paved the foundation for the organisation. This collective experience ultimately led to the establishment of Sachcha Prayas Sanstha in collaboration with Neha Sharma, a fashion designer. On November 17th, 2009, our NGO was officially established under the Society Act. Our journey began in Harduli-Mankhedi (Harduli-Bargi Gram Panchayat), Jabalpur district, Madhya Pradesh. This area witnessed significant displacement of marginalised communities due to the Bargi Dam construction. In the beginning, our main goal was to empower rural women, especially those in distress, through skill-building, that could help them live dignified and financially independent lives. These women often face challenges and are denied basic rights, even though they shoulder most of the responsibilities of their households and farms. Currently, we have expanded our organisation's capabilities to simultaneously focus on child rights, education, legal aid and health. We understand that empowerment goes beyond words â€“ it's about skills that translate into self-reliance. Our focus has been to empower women with skills such as machine tailoring, hand embroidery, mehndi art, and beauty parlour techniques. By providing these comprehensive skill-set training along with encouraging traditional forms occupations, we enable women to contribute to their households and communities positively. Our core purpose is to create a better future for those often left behind, particularly rural women and children. Our biggest aspiration is to empower displaced families, adivasis, SC, ST, and backward-class individuals by providing them with valuable skills sets and opportunities.

Mission

Our mission is to empower marginalised segments of society, with a special emphasis on women and children. We are dedicated to helping them access knowledge and information, equipping them to lead empowered and dignified lives.

Vision

We envision a society where every individual, with a special focus on women and children, experiences a life of dignity, enjoying equitable access to essential services encompassing health, education, and basic needs.

Objective

Our project aspires to cultivate empowered women, girls and children through the promotion of income-generating skills, raising awareness, and improving access to basic rights. Our ultimate goal is to nurture a sense of environmental security, enhance overall health, and promote legal literacy. Through these endeavours, we aim to contribute to the creation of a community that is both sustainable and empowered.
