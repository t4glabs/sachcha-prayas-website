Our Origin and Commitment

Sachcha Prayas Sanstha's journey traces back to the idea of providing tailoring training in Gram Panchayat Harduli Bargi Nagar during 2007-2008. Officially registered under the Society Act on November 17, 2009, our organisation embarked on its mission with a steadfast commitment despite initial resource limitations. Founded by Mr. Mohd Parvez Khan, Sachcha Prayas Sanstha is rooted in his personal experiences as part of a displaced family. Motivated by his journalistic endeavours, Parvez established the organisation to uplift rural women and address their unique challenges. Our mission centres around empowering women in rural areas, equipping them with skills to earn a livelihood within their communities. By enhancing economic autonomy and recognition, we aim to alleviate the burden often borne by women in household and agricultural responsibilities.

Our humble origins never deterred us from dreaming big and creating more pathways for impact. Despite initial scepticism and lack of interest, we kick-started our livelihood initiative with a batch of 10 women (mostly school dropouts). During this period, we encountered challenges such as the absence of proper guidance, economic resources, and basic infrastructure. We also realised that the free training garnered limited interest due to the widespread notion that free programmes offered no value. We introduced a nominal registration fee, which increased the participation rate in the programme. Currently, the batch size is between 30-40 participants. The support from other organisations and the community, helped SPS overcome the initial hurdles. Inspired by our dedication, social workers from in and around the Gargi region rallied together and donated an old sewing machine and seating facilities in the Anganwadi centre. Further Smt. Usha Vinodia and the officers of the Gargi Women and Child Development Department donated furniture and sewing machines. As SPSâ€™s work garnered more interest and visibility, we approached the Bargi Dam Irrigation Department authorities to build a centre to facilitate the livelihood programme. After a year of bureaucratic hurdles and other challenges, the authorities allocated a spacious hall on a monthly rental basis. The hall lacked basic amenities such as an electricity connection and furniture. Social workers in the region rallied together and raised financial aid through crowdfunding. This fund was used to set up the office and the livelihood training centre. Over the years, the organisation has grown and diversified its offerings to include a range of social welfare programmes (e.g. Rural Panchayat initiatives, legal aid, etc.).

Currently, Sachcha Prayas Sanstha continues to focus on women's empowerment through livelihood training. The livelihood training has expanded to include hand embroidery and beautician skills. Along with other rural panchayat initiatives surrounding education and health, we have also ventured into legal aid, with a focus on child rights advocacy. Since our inception in 2009, we have faced many hurdles. Currently, our challenges include: Lack/Shortage of technical staff Shortage of resources Regular & reliable funding The immediate projected goal for the organisation is to make 200 women self-reliant through employment-oriented initiatives and create a positive atmosphere for child rights advocacy in at least 10 Gram Panchayats.
