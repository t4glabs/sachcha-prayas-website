Our Team

  • Parwez Khan

    Born into a traditional landlord family in Mankedi village, Jabalpur, Madhya Pradesh, Parvez Khan's life took an unexpected turn due to the construction of the Bargi Dam in 1981. The displacement his family faced transformed them from landlords to displaced individuals, shaping Parvez's path towards making a meaningful impact. After completing his degree in journalism and mass communication, Parvez chose rural journalism as his path. He used the media as a platform to shed light on issues related to water, forests, land, and displacement in rural areas. Mr. Khan also pursued a master's degree in rural development and decided to forgo traditional employment opportunities. Parvez often observed that rural women were engaged in labour-intensive tasks like collecting wood, tending to cattle, and agricultural work. This inspired him to envision a way to help them gain skills that would enable them to lead financially independent lives with dignity. Teaming up with Neha Sharma, a friend passionate about dress designing and women's issues, he established a training centre. Here, courses in sewing, embroidery, and beauty skills were offered, starting with a modest 15-women cohort within an Anganwadi. Parvez's initiative garnered attention from the Women and Child Development Department, marking the birth of Sachcha Prayas. This organisation aimed to uplift rural women through skill development and empowerment. Overcoming resource constraints, Parvez's unwavering determination brought to life an organisation poised to transform the lives of women in his community.

  • Neha Sharma

    Neha Sharma, a dedicated social worker, embarked on her journey in social service as a founding member of SPS. Over time, she assumed the role of secretary within the organisation. While pursuing her fashion design degree and continuing her studies at home, she struck a friendship with the organisation's founder, Parvez Khan. Their shared educational background and camaraderie ignited a vision to empower rural women. Together, they launched a sewing training program for women, initially starting with a humble banner in her sister's Anganwadi building. Neha not only imparts employment-oriented training to women but actively engages other spheres of social service. Armed with a degree in social work, she has dedicated 14 years to serving various facets of social welfare. Her involvement spans crucial government initiatives, including the Madhya Pradesh government's Ayushman Bharat Yojana and Ladli Bahna Yojana. She plays a pivotal role in facilitating online services for beneficiaries in Gram Panchayats through Common Service Centers. Furthermore, Mrs. Sharma lends her expertise as a para-legal volunteer for the Madhya Pradesh State Legal Services Authority and District Legal Services Authority, providing invaluable legal support to those in need. Presently, she spearheads self-help groups for women, fostering self-reliance, and remains passionately engaged in inspiring and managing these self-help groups.

  • Razia Mansoori

    Razia is a teacher and has been working as a guest teacher in government schools. Razia also provides free coaching to underprivileged tribal children at home. In addition to her teaching role, she has been an active member of SPS's journey for the last ten years. Her work is centred around women's empowerment and works to inspire women in the region to form self-help groups and engage in various activities that benefit the community. Razia holds a master's degree in arts and a bachelor's degree in education. Her main focus has been to actively engage in creating a positive atmosphere in the community regarding women's and children's issues.

  • Navin Shiva

    Navin Shiva is a social worker by profession, an educator, skilled speaker, trainer, and motivator who has been actively involved in the social sector for the past 15 years, consistently providing training to youth in various subjects (water conservation, the Panchayati Raj system, leadership, time management, team building, and life skills). Mr. Shiva has been actively engaged in the Nehru Yuva Kendra Sangathan under the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, Government of India, for several years and has participated in numerous programs. Similarly, he is a counsellor at the Indira Gandhi National Open University (IGNOU) under the Ministry of Education and provides guidance to students in postgraduate, undergraduate, diploma, and certificate courses in the university's social work program. Mr. Shiva is also actively involved in the ambitious government initiative "Jal Jeevan Mission" in various districts of Madhya Pradesh, playing an active role in achieving the goals of "Har Ghar Nal Har Ghar Jal" (Every Household Tap Connection and Water) in the region. Furthermore, Mr. Shiva is associated with various organisations at the national and state levels, including Vikas Samvad Bhopal, District Child Rights Forum Bhopal, Debate Bhopal, UNICEF, National Youth Center New Delhi, Rajiv Gandhi National Institute of Youth Development Chennai, and National Institute of Rural Development and Panchayati Raj Hyderabad. Mr. Shiva has been actively involved with Sacha Prayas Sanstha for the past ten years. He designs better strategies for various employment-oriented programs for effective implementation.

  • Nikhilesh Dubey

    Nikhilesh Dubey holds a Bachelor's degree in Commerce and a Master of Social Work degree, laying the foundation for his extensive 11-year journey in the social sector. Throughout his career, he has significantly contributed to diverse areas of social development, spanning health, education, addiction prevention, livelihood, environment, and gender equality. Nikhilesh has collaborated with both national and international organisations, including Population Services International (PSI), Youth for Change (YP), Samriddhi Mahila Evam Bal Vikas Kendra (SMVK), Maan Foundation, All World Gayatri Pariwar (AWGP), and Kadam Sanstha. Currently, Nikhilesh Dubey serves as the District Manager at Deepak Foundation, a prominent national organisation based in Tikamgarh, Madhya Pradesh. His journey with Sacha Prayas Sanstha began when he connected with the organisation through the Nehru Yuva Kendra Sangathan (Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, Jabalpur). Impressed by the organisation's impactful work, he has been actively engaged with SPS for the past eight years, dedicating his efforts to women's empowerment, child rights, and other societal issues. Nikhilesh also contributes his valuable experiences and insights on critical matters within the organisation.
